Safe Spacecraft Rendezvous & Proximity Operations — Penn State · Purdue · Texas A&M
Challenge: RPO missions are executed in increasingly complex, congested, and competitive environments. Spacecraft have limited maneuverability based on orbit, fuel, thrusters, and mission requirements.

Objective: Investigate computational methods enabling on-board, autonomous RPOs with collision avoidance techniques. Demonstrate efficacy via hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) experiments.

Member Benefits: High-fidelity, computationally efficient guidance and navigation laws for autonomous safe space operations, plus well-trained graduate researchers available for internships.
Active Tendons for Controlled Dimensional Stability — Penn State · Texas A&M
Challenge: In-Space Assembly and Manufacturing (ISAM) is needed for future large-aperture (≥15m) missions that cannot be launched in a single mission. Enabling concepts include additive manufacturing, modularity, and robotics.

Objective: Advance tendon actuation for precision shape maintenance and control of large tensegrity apertures and robots. Builds on DARPA Compact Hybrid Actuation Program research. Texas A&M LASR lab will test deployment.

Member Benefits: Access to developed motor, metrology, and cable-management technology, plus talented graduate researchers.
In-Space Refueling of High-Performance Storable Propellants — Purdue
Challenge: Spacecraft in cislunar space face harsh thermal environments. MMH/NTO systems are preferred for high-ΔV missions, but cryogenic propellants (LO₂/LCH₄) require expensive active cooling for long missions.

Objective: Offer an alternative using gaseous oxygen and kerosene with chemical oxygen generators for refueling — simpler than cryogenic, higher performance than traditional storables.

Member Benefits: Early involvement in technology development with ability to provide design guidelines fitting existing or planned spacecraft.
